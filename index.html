Why Yoga?

Yoga योग is the foundation on which we can reach a life of enlightenment, freedom, fearlessness, and unconditional infinite love and kindness. Yoga is for Life.

Practicing Yoga in its entirety is like eating a broad and healthy diet. The physical exercises such as Asanas आसन (postures), Pranayama प्राणायाम (breathing and energy exercises), and Mudras मुद्रा tackle chronic illness such as back pain, abdominal or sleep disorders and clear the way for Yoga’s more profound practices of Dhyana ध्यान, Dharana धारणा and Samadhi समाधि. These last three aspects of Yoga might be simplistically labelled “meditation”, and these are the only components that take us towards a new life of enlightenment.

Through meditation we can explore many things which we may not even be aware of in our current state of mind. Virtues of unconditional love, kindness, compassion and mercy.

Having built up these virtues in ourselves we can put them into action through Karma कर्म, Bhakti भक्ति and Jnana ज्ञान Yoga. Our interactions with the world through our daily work, our behaviour and our thoughts have a lasting impact on not only our lives, but the lives of those around us as well.

So Yoga is a complete system for life. Yoga is for our body, mind, and soul. Yoga is for sick people, Yoga is for healthy people and happy people. Yoga is for people of all levels. Yoga is for prenatal infants, pregnant women, newborns, children, adults, middle aged people, and elderly. Even on the deathbed, gratefulness and Yogic principles can help transition to a better state of consciousness.

Yoga can give meaning and purpose to life. Instead of mindless, mechanical eating we see the purpose, ‘I need to eat for my health and happiness so I can do good in the world and live a life of greater peace, quality, and harmony.’

At the root of Yoga is Sadhana (practice), and the fruit of Yoga is also Sadhana. This practice is the ongoing research into our lives and the meaning of our existence across all dimensions, not only the physical.
