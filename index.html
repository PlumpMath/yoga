Kamal was born in the Himalayas and has been teaching Yoga since he was 16 years old, having practiced Yoga from a very young age as taught by his father, he went on to study Yoga and philosophy at University. Since Kamal moved to London (how many years ago?) he teaches elderly in care homes, children in schools, group classes, and a few private students. Kamal has a loving wife Junco who practices and teaches yoga for natural childbirth, and together they have two children who are also keen yoga practitioners!

Kamal is very humble, and at first glance, on listening to his playful use of language, many might overlook his deep spiritual understanding of Yoga. He speaks 6 languages, is learning Japanese, and is extremely well read in ancient Sanskrit texts as well as classical western literature. He will answer your questions on deep levels that resonate with truth and insight.

Most importantly Kamal practices what he teaches. He is loving and compassionate, a quality one feels as soon as they meet him, he never exudes anger or fear and he is very open and asking of criticism to improve himself. His teaching and practice of yoga is of much more than the Asanas (postures) that we might associate with Yoga, they extend to every aspect of life. He has travelled and learned from many great masters of the Himalayas, but has left the peaceful retreats of the hermitages and caves where other yogis dwell, and thrown himself in the deep end, to help those most in need of his teachings. He has helped reform people who would sleep all day, people with alcohol or drug problems, and people who had lost all hope. It would be his greatest joy to really help just 5 people in his lifetime.

Like the 'Lotus' flower that his name signifies in Sanskrit, Kamal is bright and cheerful amid the greys of our modern London life; he brings happiness and joy to those around him and I am so grateful to have him as my teacher.
